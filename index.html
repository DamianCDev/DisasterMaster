Prepare for a Bushfire

Before a fire (wildfire) you should:
  • Build an emergency Kit with First Aid essentials included
  • Design your home to be fire repellant - taking into consideration plants and materials
  • Regularily clean gutters and roof of flamable material such as sticks.
  • Install Smoke Alarms (forboth household and Wild fires.)
during the fire stay away from the front and arrange temporary housing elseware if instructed by authorities.

Prepare for a Storm

Before a storm you should:
  • Build an emergency Kit with First Aid essentials included
  • Position yourself inside a home, building or hardtop automobile (not a convertible!)
  • Unplug any electronic equiptment well before the strom hits your region
During the storm makesure that you stay inside and in a sheltered area and avoid contact with plugged in electronics

Prepare for a Flood

During a flood you should:
  • Secure your home, if you have time.
  • Turn off utilities at the main switches or valves
  • Do not walk through moving water. Only six inches of moving water can make you fall

Prepare for a Cyclone

To prepare for a cyclone, you should take the following measures:
  • Know your surroundings
  • Identify hazards such as trees and water bodies and determined if they pose a threat to you
  • Cover your houses windows
During a cyclone:
  • Listen to the radio/TV for information
  • Secure your home and outside objects
  • Avoid using the phone, except for emergencies

Prepare for a Earthquake

Before an earthquake you should probably:
  • Secure items that could fall and cause injuries
  • Store critical supplies and documents
During an Earthquake you need to:
  • Stay where you are until the shaking stops
  • Do not run outside
  • Drop down onto your hands and knees

Prepare for an Epidemic/Pandemic

To prepare for an epidemic:
  • Store a 2 week supply of water and food
  • Make sure you have a plentiful supply of other important items, like personal and prescription drugs
